================================================================================
DOLE LABOR ADVISORY NO. 08, SERIES OF 2023
SAFETY AND HEALTH MEASURES TO PREVENT AND CONTROL HEAT STRESS
AT THE WORKPLACE
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Labor Advisory No. 08, Series of 2023 (LA 08-23) provides safety and health measures to prevent and control heat stress at the workplace, pursuant to Article 168 of the Labor Code.

================================================================================
SECTION 1: COVERAGE
================================================================================

This Advisory covers:
(a) All employers in the private sector
(b) All workplaces in the private sector
(c) For the benefit of all employees

================================================================================
SECTION 2: HEALTH RISKS OF HEAT STRESS
================================================================================

Heat-related illnesses include:
(a) Heat stroke (medical emergency)
(b) Heat exhaustion
(c) Heat cramps
(d) Heat rash
(e) Fainting
(f) Dehydration

Risk factors include:
(a) High temperature and humidity
(b) Direct sun exposure
(c) Physical exertion
(d) Lack of acclimatization
(e) Certain medications
(f) Pre-existing health conditions (e.g., hypertension, kidney diseases)

================================================================================
SECTION 3: REQUIRED CONTROL MEASURES
================================================================================

Employers are required to adopt measures designed to minimize risks associated with heat exposure:

3.1 Risk Assessment:
"Assess the risk of exposure of workers to extreme heat vis-Ã -vis their existing comorbidities (e.g., hypertension and kidney diseases)"

3.2 Engineering Controls:
(a) Elimination of heat and humidity in workplaces through effective ventilation
(b) Heat insulation in all areas with workers (e.g., General Exhaust Ventilation)
(c) Air conditioning where feasible
(d) Use of fans and cooling systems
(e) Reflective barriers or shields

3.3 Administrative Controls:
(a) Adjustment of rest breaks
(b) Work location changes to allow recovery from heat exposure
(c) Job rotation to reduce continuous heat exposure
(d) Modified work schedules during extreme heat

3.4 Personal Protection:
(a) Temperature-appropriate uniforms
(b) Appropriate personal protective equipment
(c) Cooling vests or gear where applicable

3.5 Hydration:
(a) Provide workers with free and adequate drinking water
(b) Accessible at all times
(c) Encourage frequent water intake
(d) Electrolyte replacement drinks when appropriate

================================================================================
SECTION 4: FLEXIBLE WORK ARRANGEMENTS
================================================================================

Employers, with the concurrence of workers, are urged to adopt flexible work arrangements that:
(a) Allow work hours adjustments
(b) Maintain the total number of work hours within the day or week
(c) Continue until weather conditions have improved

Examples:
- Earlier start times
- Extended lunch breaks during hottest hours
- Shift adjustments
- Work-from-home arrangements where feasible

================================================================================
SECTION 5: EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
================================================================================

"Establish procedures and information networks to address heat-related emergencies."

Emergency procedures shall include:
(a) Recognition of heat illness symptoms
(b) First aid measures
(c) Emergency contact information
(d) Transportation to medical facilities
(e) Communication protocols

Symptoms to recognize:
- Confusion or altered mental state
- Slurred speech
- Loss of consciousness
- Hot, dry skin or excessive sweating
- Seizures
- Very high body temperature

================================================================================
SECTION 6: AWARENESS AND TRAINING
================================================================================

Conduct advocacy campaigns on:
(a) Identifying symptoms of heat stress
(b) Addressing symptoms at the workplace
(c) Prevention measures
(d) First aid for heat-related illnesses
(e) Personal hydration and nutrition

================================================================================
SECTION 7: TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE
================================================================================

Employers are encouraged to coordinate with:
(a) DOLE Regional Offices
(b) Occupational Safety and Health Center-Regional Extension Units

For technical assistance in:
(a) Reviewing safety and health measures
(b) Developing heat stress prevention programs
(c) Assessing workplace heat hazards

================================================================================
SECTION 8: EMPLOYER RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

Employers shall:
(a) Assess heat hazards in the workplace
(b) Implement appropriate control measures
(c) Provide adequate drinking water
(d) Allow rest breaks
(e) Train supervisors and workers
(f) Respond to heat emergencies
(g) Monitor weather conditions
(h) Document heat-related incidents

================================================================================
SECTION 9: WORKER RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

Workers shall:
(a) Stay hydrated
(b) Take authorized breaks
(c) Report symptoms early
(d) Follow safety procedures
(e) Look out for co-workers
(f) Use provided protective equipment

================================================================================
SECTION 10: MONITORING AND COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

DOLE regional offices shall:
(a) Monitor compliance during inspections
(b) Provide technical assistance
(c) Issue advisories as needed
(d) Coordinate with OSHC

================================================================================
SECTION 11: RELATED ISSUANCES
================================================================================

This Advisory should be read with:
(a) Rule 1070 of OSHS (Environmental Control)
(b) Rule 1960 of OSHS (Occupational Health Services)
(c) RA 11058 (OSH Law)
(d) DO 252-25 (Revised IRR)
