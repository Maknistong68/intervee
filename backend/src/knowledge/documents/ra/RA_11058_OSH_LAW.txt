================================================================================
REPUBLIC ACT NO. 11058
AN ACT STRENGTHENING COMPLIANCE WITH OCCUPATIONAL SAFETY AND HEALTH STANDARDS
AND PROVIDING PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS THEREOF
Approved: August 17, 2018
================================================================================

CHAPTER I
DECLARATION OF POLICY

SECTION 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as the "Occupational Safety and Health Standards Act".

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. - The State affirms labor as a primary social economic force and that a safe and healthy workforce is an integral aspect of nation building. Towards this end, the State shall ensure a safe and healthful workplace for all working people by affording them full protection against all hazards in their work environment.

The State shall ensure that the provisions of the Labor Code of the Philippines, as amended, all domestic laws, and internationally-recognized standards on occupational safety and health (OSH) are being fully enforced and complied with by employers. The State shall undertake capacity building programs for employers, workers and their organizations, and other stakeholders in cooperation with the Occupational Safety and Health Center (OSHC).

In line with these policies, the Department of Labor and Employment shall have the sole and exclusive jurisdiction over the implementation of the occupational safety and health standards.

================================================================================
CHAPTER II
GENERAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

SECTION 3. Coverage. - This Act shall apply to all establishments, projects, sites including Philippine Economic Zone Authority (PEZA) and all other Special Economic Zone as may be created by law, and all other places where work is being undertaken in all branches of economic activity, except in the public sector. For the purpose of this Act, the term "all branches of economic activity" shall include production, construction, transportation, commerce and services.

The coverage and application of this Act shall not include:
(a) Public sector employees which shall be covered by rules and regulations issued by the Civil Service Commission (CSC), in consultation with the Department of Health (DOH), for workers in the government, including government-owned and controlled corporations with original charters;
(b) For workers in the shipping and maritime industry, the coverage and application of this Act shall be governed by the applicable international conventions ratified by the Philippines, applicable domestic labor laws, Presidential Decree No. 442, as amended, and other related issuances of the Maritime Industry Authority (MARINA) and the Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE); and
(c) For workers in the mining industry, the mining safety and health standards promulgated by the Mines and Geosciences Bureau (MGB) of the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR).

SECTION 4. Definition of Terms. - As used in this Act:
(a) Certified First-aider - any person trained and duly certified as qualified to administer first aid by the Philippine Red Cross or any organization authorized by the Bureau;
(b) Competency Standard - a written description of the knowledge, skills, and attitudes, including values, expected of a worker in order to successfully perform in the workplace consistent with established standards;
(c) Contractor - any person or entity engaged in a legitimate contracting or subcontracting arrangement providing either services, labor, or materials, or the sale or installation of equipment to its clients;
(d) Employer - any person acting directly or indirectly in the interest of an employer in relation to a worker;
(e) Equipment - shall include tool, apparatus, appliance, machine, device, and personal protective equipment used to perform work activity;
(f) Hazardous Waste - a waste substance or mixture of waste substances which possesses the characteristics or exhibits any of the characteristics of ignitability, corrosivity, reactivity, or toxicity;
(g) Hazardous Work - any activity or operation which exposes a worker to danger or environmental elements from the work site where he or she works;
(h) Health - a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being;
(i) Imminent Danger - a condition or practice in any workplace which could reasonably be expected to cause death or serious physical harm before abatement;
(j) Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) - enterprises based on the criteria set by Republic Act No. 9501, otherwise known as the "Magna Carta for Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs)";
(k) Occupational Health Personnel - a physician, dentist, nurse, and first-aider engaged on a full time or part-time or as consultant in an establishment duly licensed and registered in accordance with existing laws;
(l) Occupational Safety and Health Standards (OSHS) - standards that set the minimum requirements for the employer to provide a safe and healthful working environment, and shall include provisions relating to regulations on working conditions, wage protection and related topics;
(m) Occupational Sickness/Illness - any illness or disorder, which is caused by or arises out of the nature and condition of the work activities or working environment and involves gradual health deterioration resulting from exposure to work-related hazard as part of normal work activity;
(n) Safe Work - work performed in a hazard-free environment in accordance with set occupational safety and health standards;
(o) Safety Officer - any employee who by training and experience is competent to identify hazards in the workplace and recommend corrective measure as certified through the Bureau of Working Conditions (BWC); and
(p) Worker - any person engaged or suffered to work for an employer for compensation.

================================================================================
CHAPTER III
DUTIES OF EMPLOYERS, WORKERS, AND OTHER PERSONS
================================================================================

SECTION 5. Duties of Employers, Contractors and Subcontractors. - The employer, contractor or subcontractor, if any, and any person who manages, controls or supervises the work being undertaken shall have the duty to:
(a) Furnish the workers a place of employment free from hazardous conditions that are causing or likely to cause death, illness or physical harm to the workers;
(b) Give complete job safety instructions or orientation to all the workers, especially to those entering the job for the first time, including those relating to familiarization with their work environment, hazards to which workers are exposed to and steps to take in case of emergency;
(c) Inform the workers of the hazards associated with their work, health risks involved or to which they are exposed to, preventive measures to eliminate or minimize the risks, and steps to be taken in cases of emergency;
(d) Use only approved devices and equipment for the workplace;
(e) Comply with OSH standards including training, medical examination and, where necessary, provisions on protective and safety devices such as personal protective equipment (PPE) and machine guards;
(f) Allow workers and their safety and health representatives to actively participate in the process of organizing, planning, implementing and evaluating the safety and health program;
(g) Provide, where necessary, for measures to deal with emergencies and accidents including first-aid arrangements;
(h) Make arrangements for workers and safety and health representatives to have the time and resource to participate actively in the process of organizing, planning and implementation, monitoring, evaluation and action for improvement of the OSH management system;
(i) Ensure that workers and their safety and health representatives are consulted, informed and trained on all aspects of OSH including emergency arrangements, associated with their work;
(j) Comply with this Act, all its implementing rules and regulations (IRR), all DOLE issuances, including other related laws;
(k) Provide safety signage at work sites to warn workers and the public of immediately dangerous conditions, areas, or materials; and
(l) Provide welfare facilities and, where applicable, accessible facilities for persons with disability.

SECTION 6. Duties of Workers. - Workers shall:
(a) Participate in capacity building activities on safety and health, and other OSH-related topics;
(b) Make proper use of all safeguards and safety devices furnished for their protection and that of others, and observe instructions made to provide for their own safety;
(c) Observe prescribed steps to be taken in case of emergency; and
(d) Report immediately to their superior and/or to the Safety Officer any work hazard that may be discovered in the workplace.

SECTION 7. Rights of Workers. - Workers shall be entitled to the following rights:
(a) The right to be informed of work hazards, given instructions and training on safe work practices and the use of PPE;
(b) Right to demand from their employers an OSH program and complete job safety instructions;
(c) Right to refuse unsafe work if an imminent danger situation exists in the workplace that may result in illness, injury or death, which shall not be grounds for the termination of employment or discrimination against workers. In cases where an imminent danger situation exists in the workplace, the worker has a right not to return to the work area until such situation has been corrected;
(d) Right to report accidents and dangerous occurrences to the employer, without fear of reprisal from the employer or any person in authority;
(e) Right to personal protective equipment (PPE);
(f) Right to be provided appropriate first aid treatment and medical services, where applicable; and
(g) Right to be compensated for work-related illness and injuries pursuant to existing laws.

================================================================================
CHAPTER IV
COVERED WORKPLACES
================================================================================

SECTION 8. Establishments Covered. - Every employer, contractor, or subcontractor, if any, shall establish in his or her workplace program compliant with OSH standards as required by this Act and its IRR.

SECTION 9. Safety and Health Information. - Employers shall comply with the duty to inform the workers on their rights under this Act and its IRR or under a valid collective bargaining agreement (CBA) in the workplace, including but not limited to the safety and health program, hazards at the workplace, and health safety of workers.

SECTION 10. Occupational Safety and Health Standards. - DOLE shall set mandatory OSH standards that provide minimum requirements for the protection of the safety and health of workers through effective implementation, compliance, and enforcement.

SECTION 11. Safety and Health Committee. - Every workplace shall establish a health and safety committee as required by this Act and its IRR.

SECTION 12. Workers' Welfare Facilities. - All establishments shall be provided with welfare facilities as required by this Act and its IRR.

SECTION 13. Occupational Health Personnel. - The requirements for occupational health personnel shall be in accordance with this Act and its IRR.

SECTION 14. Required Training and Accreditation. - All covered workplaces shall ensure that their responsible safety and health officers have undertaken the required trainings and are accredited.

SECTION 15. Emergency and First Aid Kits. - The employer shall provide adequate first aid facilities that include a clinic, emergency equipment and trained personnel, as required by this Act and its IRR.

================================================================================
CHAPTER V
JOINT AND SOLIDARY LIABILITY
================================================================================

SECTION 16. Shared Responsibility on Occupational Safety and Health. - The employer, project owner, general contractor, contractor or subcontractor, if any, and any person who manages, controls or supervises the work being undertaken shall be jointly and solidarily liable for compliance with this Act.

================================================================================
CHAPTER VI
COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT
================================================================================

SECTION 17. Authority. - The Secretary of Labor and Employment and his or her authorized representative shall have access to enter any workplace, to conduct inspection on compliance with OSH Standards and to access employer's records for OSH-related information.

SECTION 18. Visitorial and Enforcement Authority. - The Secretary of Labor and Employment or his or her duly authorized representative shall have access to employer's records and premises at any time of the day or night whenever work is being undertaken therein, and the right to:
(a) Inspect the work premises to enforce the provisions of this Act and its IRR;
(b) Copy from such employer's records relevant to OSH compliance;
(c) Question any employee;
(d) Investigate any fact, condition or matter that may be necessary to determine compliance or violations including OSH-related investigations; and
(e) Order stoppage of work or suspension of operations in any unit of the workplace, where noncompliance with the law or implementing rules poses grave and imminent danger to the health and safety of the workers: Provided, That a hearing shall be conducted, within twenty-four (24) hours from the issuance of the order, to determine whether the cause of the work stoppage can be corrected without totally shutting down the operation.

The visitorial and enforcement power of the Secretary of Labor and Employment, or his or her authorized representative, shall only be exercised during regular working hours or the hours during which the employees are at work.

SECTION 19. Setting of Standards. - The Secretary of Labor and Employment shall set the standards and/or amendments through board resolutions under the Tripartite Industrial Peace Council (TIPC) based on the guidelines set in this Act and shall issue rules and regulations for the effective implementation of the provisions of this Act. The standards and amendments issued shall take into account:
(a) Substantial differences in hazards among geographic areas;
(b) Substantial differences in scale of operations or processes;
(c) Type of risk involved or the degree of exposure to hazard in various industries; and
(d) Reasonable period for compliance and technical capability of the workplace.

SECTION 20. Enforcement. - All covered workplaces are required to comply with the provisions of this Act and its IRR within six (6) months from the IRR's effectivity.

For establishments that fail to comply with the provisions of this Act and its IRR and all OSH standards, the following schedule of fines shall be applied without prejudice to the accessory penalties stated therein, applicable criminal penalties, and civil action, if any:
(a) For establishments found to have committed willful violation of this Act and its IRR: a daily penalty of One hundred thousand pesos (P100,000.00) shall be imposed until such violation has been corrected.
(b) For establishments found to have committed violations of this Act and its IRR through fault or negligence, after determination of the establishment's financial capacity to pay, a graduated schedule of fines shall be imposed.

An employer or any person in authority in the workplace who fails or refuses to comply with this Act, any standard, rule or order issued pursuant to this Act, shall be administratively liable for the amount to be paid to each worker in an amount equivalent to three (3) month salary per day, starting from the time the imminent danger situation has been brought to the attention of such employer, until the hazard has been corrected.

SECTION 21. Appeals. - Any person who is aggrieved by the decision or order of the DOLE Regional Director may file an appeal to the Office of the Secretary within ten (10) days from receipt of the decision or order.

================================================================================
CHAPTER VII
MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS
================================================================================

SECTION 22. Computerized Compliance System. - DOLE shall institute a computerized system of monitoring compliance with OSH standards for the efficiency and effectivity of enforcement.

SECTION 23. Multi-employer Worksites. - Where two (2) or more employers share a single worksite, they shall cooperate in applying the OSH requirements provided in this Act and its IRR.

SECTION 24. Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprise (MSME). - DOLE shall formulate the necessary rules and regulations for the specific requirements of MSMEs to ensure compliance with OSH requirements, with attention to the capacity of such enterprises.

SECTION 25. Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR). - Within sixty (60) days from the effectivity of this Act, the DOLE, in consultation with employers' and workers' organizations, and concerned government agencies, shall formulate the necessary rules and regulations for its effective implementation.

SECTION 26. Coordination with Other Government Agencies. - DOLE shall coordinate with other government agencies involved in OSH for the effective implementation of this Act and its IRR.

SECTION 27. Separability Clause. - If any portion or provision of this Act is declared invalid or unconstitutional, the remaining portions or provisions hereof shall remain valid and effective.

SECTION 28. Repealing Clause. - All laws, presidential decrees, executive orders, rules and regulations, or parts thereof, which are inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed, amended, or modified accordingly.

SECTION 29. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its complete publication in the Official Gazette or in any newspaper of general circulation.
