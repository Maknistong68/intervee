================================================================================
DOLE DEPARTMENT ORDER NO. 252, SERIES OF 2025
REVISED IMPLEMENTING RULES AND REGULATIONS (IRR) OF REPUBLIC ACT NO. 11058
"An Act Strengthening Compliance with Occupational Safety and Health Standards
and Providing Penalties for Violations Thereof"
Effective: May 16, 2025
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Department Order No. 252, Series of 2025 (DO 252-25) formally adopts the Revised Implementing Rules and Regulations of Republic Act No. 11058. This supersedes Department Order No. 198-18.

DO 252-25 took effect on May 16, 2025 and strengthens workplace safety regulations, ensuring that businesses are better equipped to protect workers in an evolving economic landscape.

================================================================================
CHAPTER I: GENERAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

SECTION 1. Title and Scope

This Order shall be known as the "Revised Implementing Rules and Regulations of Republic Act No. 11058."

SECTION 2. Coverage

The revised IRR covers:
(a) All establishments, projects, and work sites, regardless of industry or location
(b) Philippine Economic Zone Authority (PEZA) zones
(c) All other Special Economic Zones
(d) All places where work is being undertaken

Expanded Coverage under DO 252-25:
(a) Residences converted into workplaces
(b) Government-owned or controlled corporations (GOCCs) without original charter
(c) Economic zone enterprises
(d) Contracting and subcontracting activities in both private and public sectors
(e) Modern work arrangements including remote work setups

SECTION 3. Exclusions

The following are excluded:
(a) Public sector employees covered by CSC rules
(b) Shipping and maritime industry workers (covered by MARINA/ILO conventions)
(c) Mining industry workers (covered by MGB/DENR)

================================================================================
CHAPTER II: DUTIES AND RIGHTS
================================================================================

SECTION 4. Duties of Employers

Employers shall:
(a) Provide a workplace free from hazardous conditions
(b) Give complete job safety instructions and orientation
(c) Inform workers of hazards and health risks
(d) Use only approved devices and equipment
(e) Comply with all OSH standards and training requirements
(f) Allow worker participation in OSH programs
(g) Provide emergency and first aid arrangements
(h) Ensure workers are consulted on all OSH matters
(i) Provide safety signage at work sites
(j) Provide welfare facilities and accessible facilities for PWDs

SECTION 5. Duties of Workers

Workers shall:
(a) Participate in capacity building activities
(b) Make proper use of all safeguards and safety devices
(c) Observe prescribed emergency steps
(d) Report hazards immediately to supervisor or Safety Officer

SECTION 6. Rights of Workers

Workers are entitled to:
(a) Right to information on work hazards
(b) Right to demand OSH program and safety instructions
(c) Right to refuse unsafe work without fear of reprisal
(d) Right to report accidents without reprisal
(e) Right to personal protective equipment (free of charge)
(f) Right to first aid treatment and medical services
(g) Right to compensation for work-related illness/injuries

SECTION 7. Right to Refuse Work

Employees/workers have the right to refuse work without threat or reprisal from the employer if an imminent danger situation exists in the workplace.

Safety officers may, following their determination and without fear of reprisal, implement a work stoppage or suspend operations in case an imminent danger exists in the workplace.

================================================================================
CHAPTER III: COVERED WORKPLACES
================================================================================

SECTION 8. OSH Program Requirements

Every employer shall establish an OSH Program that includes:
(a) Company OSH policy
(b) Hazard identification and risk assessment
(c) Control measures
(d) Emergency preparedness and response
(e) OSH training and education
(f) Health programs
(g) PPE provision
(h) Reporting and investigation procedures
(i) OSH committee organization

SECTION 9. Safety and Health Committee

Every workplace shall establish a health and safety committee as required by Rule 1040 of the OSHS.

SECTION 10. Safety Officers

Updated safety officer competencies under DO 252-25:
(a) Specialized training for high-risk industries
(b) Training for emerging hazards
(c) Digital workplace safety considerations
(d) Psychosocial hazard identification

SECTION 11. Occupational Health Personnel

Requirements as per Rule 1960 of the OSHS, with updated ratios.

SECTION 12. Workers' Welfare Facilities

All establishments shall provide:
(a) Adequate toilet facilities
(b) Washing facilities
(c) Changing rooms (where necessary)
(d) Rest areas
(e) Drinking water
(f) Canteen or eating areas (for larger establishments)

================================================================================
CHAPTER IV: SPECIAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

SECTION 13. Universal Health Care Integration

One of the most notable developments under DO 252-25 is the integration of access to Universal Health Care (UHC) into the occupational safety framework.

Workers shall have access to:
(a) DOH-related hospitals
(b) Rural health units
(c) PhilHealth services
(d) Mental health facilities

SECTION 14. Psychosocial Hazards

DO 252-25 incorporates a holistic risk management strategy addressing psychosocial hazards:
(a) Identification of work-related stress
(b) Bullying prevention
(c) Harassment prevention
(d) Other psychosocial risk mitigation

================================================================================
CHAPTER V: REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

SECTION 15. Required Reports

Employers must submit periodic reports through the DOLE Online Compliance Portal or relevant DOLE regional office:

(a) Annual Medical Report (AMR):
    - Due: March 31 following the reporting year
    - Contents: Health surveillance data, disease cases, mental health cases

(b) Annual Work Accident/Illness Exposure Data (AEDR):
    - Due: January 30 following the reporting year
    - Must be submitted regardless of incident occurrence

(c) Employer's Work Accident/Illness Report (WAIR):
    - Due: 30th of every month
    - Must be submitted regardless of whether incidents occurred

SECTION 16. Online Compliance Portal

All reports may be submitted through:
(a) DOLE Online Compliance Portal (reports.dole.gov.ph)
(b) Relevant DOLE Regional Office

================================================================================
CHAPTER VI: ENFORCEMENT
================================================================================

SECTION 17. Visitorial Authority

DOLE labor inspectors have:
(a) Access to employer premises at any time work is being undertaken
(b) Right to inspect workplace for compliance
(c) Right to copy relevant records
(d) Right to question employees
(e) Right to investigate any fact, condition, or matter

SECTION 18. Work Stoppage Authority

DOLE may order work stoppage when:
(a) Noncompliance poses grave and imminent danger
(b) Imminent danger to health and safety of workers
(c) Death or serious injury has occurred

A hearing shall be conducted within 24 hours from issuance of work stoppage order.

SECTION 19. Streamlined Enforcement Powers

Under DO 252-25, DOLE labor inspectors have:
(a) Clear protocols for immediate work stoppage in cases of imminent danger
(b) Streamlined enforcement procedures
(c) Enhanced coordination with other agencies

================================================================================
CHAPTER VII: PENALTIES
================================================================================

SECTION 20. Administrative Fines

Key penalty provisions under DO 252-25:

(a) Willful Non-Compliance:
    - Daily penalty of P100,000.00 until violation is corrected

(b) Progressive Fines:
    - First offense: Lower initial fine
    - Second offense: Higher fine
    - Third offense: Maximum fine

(c) Fault or Negligence:
    - Graduated schedule based on establishment's financial capacity

SECTION 21. Other Penalties

In addition to administrative fines:
(a) Work stoppage orders
(b) Compliance orders
(c) Accessory penalties
(d) Criminal penalties as applicable
(e) Civil liability

================================================================================
CHAPTER VIII: MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS
================================================================================

SECTION 22. MSMEs

DOLE shall formulate specific rules for Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) ensuring compliance with OSH requirements considering their capacity.

SECTION 23. Multi-employer Worksites

Where two or more employers share a worksite, they shall cooperate in applying OSH requirements.

SECTION 24. Transitional Provisions

All establishments must comply with DO 252-25 within six (6) months from effectivity.

SECTION 25. Repealing Clause

Department Order No. 198, Series of 2018 is hereby superseded.

================================================================================
KEY CHANGES FROM DO 198-18 TO DO 252-25
================================================================================

1. EXPANDED COVERAGE
   - Explicit inclusion of modern work arrangements
   - Remote work setups and converted home offices
   - GOCCs without original charter

2. PSYCHOSOCIAL HAZARDS
   - Mandatory identification and mitigation of stress
   - Bullying and harassment prevention requirements
   - Mental health integration

3. UNIVERSAL HEALTH CARE
   - Integration with UHC framework
   - Access to DOH facilities
   - PhilHealth coverage alignment

4. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
   - Updated deadlines
   - Mandatory monthly reporting regardless of incidents
   - Online portal submission

5. ENFORCEMENT
   - Streamlined work stoppage procedures
   - Clear imminent danger protocols
   - Enhanced inspector authority

6. PENALTIES
   - Progressive fine structure
   - Stricter accountability for repeated violations
   - P100,000 daily fine for willful non-compliance

================================================================================
EFFECTIVITY
================================================================================

This Department Order took effect on May 16, 2025.

Signed on April 28, 2025 during the World Day for Safety and Health at Work celebration at the Occupational Safety and Health Center (OSHC), Quezon City.
