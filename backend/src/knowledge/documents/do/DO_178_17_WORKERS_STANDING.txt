================================================================================
DOLE DEPARTMENT ORDER NO. 178, SERIES OF 2017
SAFETY AND HEALTH MEASURES FOR WORKERS WHO, BY THE NATURE OF THEIR WORK,
HAVE TO STAND AT WORK
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Department Order No. 178, Series of 2017 (DO 178-17) establishes safety and health measures for workers who, by the nature of their work, are required to stand for long periods or frequently walk.

================================================================================
CHAPTER I: GENERAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

SECTION 1. Title

This Order shall be known as the "Safety and Health Measures for Workers Who, By the Nature of Their Work, Have to Stand at Work."

SECTION 2. Legal Basis

Issued pursuant to Article 168 of the Labor Code, as renumbered, authorizing the Secretary of Labor and Employment to:
(a) Set and enforce mandatory occupational safety and health standards
(b) Eliminate health risks
(c) Ensure safe and healthful working conditions

SECTION 3. Coverage

This Order applies to workers who have to stand for long periods or frequently walk, such as:
(a) Retail employees
(b) Service employees
(c) Assembly line workers
(d) Teachers
(e) Security personnel
(f) Healthcare workers
(g) Food service workers
(h) Factory workers
(i) Other similar occupations

================================================================================
CHAPTER II: HEALTH RISKS OF PROLONGED STANDING
================================================================================

SECTION 4. Identified Health Risks

Prolonged standing at work can cause:
(a) Musculoskeletal disorders
(b) Lower back pain
(c) Leg muscle fatigue
(d) Leg swelling
(e) Varicose veins
(f) Neck and shoulder stiffness
(g) Foot problems
(h) Cardiovascular problems
(i) Pregnancy complications

SECTION 5. Risk Factors

Contributing factors include:
(a) Duration of standing
(b) Type of floor surface
(c) Footwear
(d) Work tasks performed
(e) Temperature and humidity
(f) Individual health conditions

================================================================================
CHAPTER III: REQUIRED CONTROL MEASURES
================================================================================

SECTION 6. Employer Duties

All employers and/or establishments are directed to institute appropriate control measures to address risks to safety and health of workers who have to stand at work.

SECTION 7. Required Measures

Employers shall implement the following:

(a) Regular Breaks:
    - Provide workers with regular breaks from standing
    - Allow sitting during breaks
    - Five-minute break every two hours from standing

(b) Seating Provisions:
    - Provide seats for workers to use during breaks
    - Allow seating when work permits
    - Provide suitable chairs or stools

(c) Floor Conditions:
    - Provide anti-fatigue mats where workers stand
    - Ensure floor surfaces are not too hard
    - Maintain clean and dry floor surfaces

(d) Footwear:
    - Allow workers to wear comfortable footwear
    - Provide or allow appropriate safety shoes
    - Ensure footwear is suitable for standing work

(e) Work Station Design:
    - Adjust work station height appropriately
    - Provide foot rails or rests
    - Ensure adequate working space

(f) Job Rotation:
    - Rotate tasks between standing and sitting work
    - Vary work activities
    - Reduce continuous standing time

(g) Training:
    - Educate workers on risks of prolonged standing
    - Train on proper posture and stretching exercises
    - Inform about early symptoms of problems

================================================================================
CHAPTER IV: ADDITIONAL MEASURES
================================================================================

SECTION 8. Optional Measures

Employers may adopt other measures such as:
(a) Compression stockings for workers with circulation issues
(b) Ergonomic assessments of work stations
(c) Health monitoring programs
(d) Exercise programs
(e) Physical therapy referrals when needed

SECTION 9. Workstation Improvements

Recommended improvements:
(a) Adjustable height work surfaces
(b) Sit-stand workstations where feasible
(c) Proper lighting to reduce strain
(d) Temperature control

================================================================================
CHAPTER V: NOTIFICATION AND COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

SECTION 10. Notification Requirements

Covered establishments must notify DOLE, through the regional office that has jurisdiction over the workplace, of their adoption of the safety and health measures.

SECTION 11. Form of Notification

The notification may be in the form of:
(a) Email
(b) Letter

Attaching the company's memo on its adapted policies of the required measures, which will be stamped as received by the DOLE officer.

SECTION 12. Inspection and Monitoring

DOLE, through its Regional Offices, is mandated to inspect and monitor the proper and strict implementation of this Order.

================================================================================
CHAPTER VI: ENFORCEMENT
================================================================================

SECTION 13. Compliance Orders

While employers may not be imprisoned for non-compliance, their failure to adopt appropriate control measures may warrant:
(a) Issuance of an Order of Compliance
(b) Inspection findings
(c) Follow-up inspections

SECTION 14. Penalties

Non-compliance may result in:
(a) Administrative penalties under OSH Standards
(b) Compliance orders
(c) Other applicable penalties under RA 11058

================================================================================
CHAPTER VII: FINAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

SECTION 15. Effectivity

This Department Order took effect on November 3, 2017.

SECTION 16. Related Issuances

This Order should be read with:
(a) Department Order No. 184, Series of 2017 (Workers Sitting at Work)
(b) Rule 1960 of OSHS (Occupational Health Services)
(c) RA 11058 (OSH Law)

================================================================================
END OF DEPARTMENT ORDER NO. 178, SERIES OF 2017
