DOLE DEPARTMENT ORDER NO. 253, SERIES OF 2025
GUIDELINES FOR THE STANDARD DESIGN AND SPECIFICATIONS FOR TEMPORARY
ACCOMMODATION AND WELFARE FACILITIES FOR CONSTRUCTION WORKERS
Signed: April 28, 2025
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Department Order No. 253, Series of 2025 (DO 253-25) provides the Guidelines for the Standard Design and Specifications for Temporary Accommodation and Welfare Facilities for Construction Workers.

This order was signed on April 28, 2025 at the Occupational Safety and Health Center (OSHC) in Quezon City, as part of the World Day for Safety and Health at Work 2025 celebration.

Theme: "Revolutionizing Health and Safety: The Role of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Digitalization at Work Towards Achieving Zero Accident"

================================================================================
CHAPTER I: GENERAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

SECTION 1. Title

This Order shall be known as the "Guidelines for the Standard Design and Specifications for Temporary Accommodation and Welfare Facilities for Construction Workers."

SECTION 2. Purpose

This Order aims to:
(a) Establish minimum standards for temporary worker housing in construction sites
(b) Ensure decent and safe living conditions for construction workers
(c) Promote the welfare and well-being of workers
(d) Complement existing OSH standards in the construction industry

SECTION 3. Coverage

This Order covers:
(a) All construction projects where temporary accommodation is provided
(b) All contractors and subcontractors providing worker housing
(c) All construction workers residing in temporary accommodations

SECTION 4. Legal Basis

Pursuant to:
(a) Republic Act No. 11058 (OSH Law)
(b) Department Order No. 13, Series of 1998 (Construction Safety)
(c) Department Order No. 252, Series of 2025 (Revised IRR of RA 11058)
(d) International Labor Organization conventions on worker welfare

================================================================================
CHAPTER II: TEMPORARY ACCOMMODATION STANDARDS
================================================================================

SECTION 5. General Requirements for Temporary Housing

All temporary accommodations shall:
(a) Be structurally safe and sound
(b) Provide protection from weather elements
(c) Have adequate ventilation and lighting
(d) Be maintained in clean and sanitary condition
(e) Be accessible to emergency services

SECTION 6. Space Requirements

Minimum Space Standards:
(a) Floor area: Minimum 4.5 square meters per worker
(b) Ceiling height: Minimum 2.4 meters
(c) Bed spacing: Minimum 1 meter between beds
(d) Aisle width: Minimum 1 meter

SECTION 7. Sleeping Quarters

Requirements for sleeping quarters:
(a) Individual beds or bunks for each worker
(b) Clean bedding materials
(c) Adequate storage space for personal belongings
(d) Privacy provisions
(e) Protection from insects and pests
(f) Separate facilities for male and female workers

SECTION 8. Ventilation and Lighting

(a) Natural ventilation through windows or openings
(b) Minimum window area: 10% of floor area
(c) Artificial ventilation where natural is inadequate
(d) Adequate lighting for safety and comfort
(e) Emergency lighting provisions

SECTION 9. Fire Safety

(a) Fire-resistant construction materials where possible
(b) Fire extinguishers readily accessible
(c) Clear and marked emergency exits
(d) Posted evacuation plans
(e) Regular fire drills
(f) Prohibition of open flames in sleeping areas

================================================================================
CHAPTER III: WELFARE FACILITIES
================================================================================

SECTION 10. Sanitary Facilities

Toilet Facilities:
(a) Minimum 1 toilet per 15 workers
(b) Separate facilities for male and female workers
(c) Properly ventilated
(d) Regularly cleaned and maintained
(e) Connected to proper waste disposal system

Washing Facilities:
(a) Adequate supply of clean water
(b) Minimum 1 wash basin per 10 workers
(c) Shower facilities with hot water where practicable
(d) Separate facilities for male and female workers

SECTION 11. Drinking Water

(a) Safe and potable drinking water
(b) Accessible at all times
(c) Sufficient quantity for all workers
(d) Properly stored and protected from contamination
(e) Regular testing for potability

SECTION 12. Dining and Kitchen Facilities

(a) Adequate dining area
(b) Clean food preparation areas
(c) Proper food storage facilities
(d) Waste disposal systems
(e) Compliance with food safety standards

SECTION 13. First Aid and Medical Facilities

(a) First aid kits readily available
(b) Trained first aiders on-site
(c) Emergency transportation arrangements
(d) Access to medical facilities
(e) Medicine storage as per Rule 1960

SECTION 14. Recreation and Rest Areas

(a) Adequate rest areas
(b) Recreation facilities where practicable
(c) Communication facilities for workers
(d) Religious or meditation areas where appropriate

================================================================================
CHAPTER IV: MAINTENANCE AND ADMINISTRATION
================================================================================

SECTION 15. Maintenance Requirements

Employers/contractors shall:
(a) Maintain all facilities in good condition
(b) Conduct regular cleaning and sanitation
(c) Perform routine inspections
(d) Make repairs promptly
(e) Control pests and vermin

SECTION 16. Administration

(a) Designate facility manager or coordinator
(b) Establish rules for facility use
(c) Maintain records of occupancy
(d) Conduct regular health and safety checks
(e) Address worker concerns promptly

SECTION 17. Worker Responsibilities

Workers shall:
(a) Maintain cleanliness of personal areas
(b) Follow facility rules
(c) Report maintenance issues
(d) Participate in safety and health activities
(e) Respect other workers' rights and privacy

================================================================================
CHAPTER V: COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT
================================================================================

SECTION 18. Compliance Requirements

(a) All covered construction projects must comply within 6 months
(b) New projects must comply from inception
(c) Existing facilities must be upgraded to meet standards

SECTION 19. Inspection

DOLE through regional offices shall:
(a) Conduct regular inspections
(b) Issue compliance orders
(c) Recommend work stoppage for serious violations
(d) Provide technical assistance

SECTION 20. Penalties

Violations shall be subject to:
(a) Administrative fines under RA 11058
(b) Compliance orders
(c) Work stoppage orders
(d) Other applicable penalties

================================================================================
CHAPTER VI: FINAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

SECTION 21. Coordination

This Order was developed in collaboration with:
(a) Bureau of Working Conditions (BWC)
(b) Philippine Domestic Construction Board (PDCB)
(c) Construction Industry Authority of the Philippines (CIAP)

SECTION 22. Technical Assistance

The DOLE OSHC Regional Extension Units shall provide technical assistance for compliance.

SECTION 23. Effectivity

This Order shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its complete publication.
