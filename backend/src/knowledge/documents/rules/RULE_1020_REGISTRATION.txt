RULE 1020: REGISTRATION

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1021: GENERAL PROVISIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1021.01 Every employer as defined in Rule 1002(1) shall register his business with the Regional Labor Office or authorized representative having jurisdiction thereof to form part of a databank of all covered establishments.

1021.02 The purpose of registration is to:
(a) Provide the Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE) with information as a guide in its enforcement activities;
(b) Establish a databank of all covered establishments for statistical and monitoring purposes;
(c) Enable DOLE to reach out to establishments for technical assistance and advisory services.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1022: REGISTRABLE UNIT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1022.01 Establishments of any size engaged in economic activity at a single location constitute one registrable unit for purposes of this regulation.

1022.02 A registrable unit shall include:
(a) Head offices;
(b) Branch offices;
(c) Project sites;
(d) Plants;
(e) Stores;
(f) Warehouses;
(g) Other physical places of business.

1022.03 Each physical place of business must be separately registered with DOLE under this Rule.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1023: PERIOD OF REGISTRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1023.01 Existing businesses must complete registration within sixty (60) days following the Standards' effective date.

1023.02 Newly established operations shall register within thirty (30) days before operation or from start-up (or resumption) of operations.

1023.03 Establishments must re-register when any of the following conditions exist:
(a) Change in business name;
(b) Change in location;
(c) Change in ownership;
(d) Reopening after previous closure.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1024: REGISTRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1024.01 Registration shall be made in form DOLE-BWC-IP-3 (OSH-RF-1020) in three copies and submitted to the Regional Labor Office or authorized representatives.

1024.02 Registration is free of charge.

1024.03 Registration remains valid for the lifetime of the establishment except when any of the following conditions exists, in which case, re-registration as if it were a new establishment is required:
(a) Change in business name;
(b) Change in location;
(c) Change in ownership;
(d) Reopening after previous closure.

1024.04 Documentation Requirements:

The following documents must be submitted:
(a) Duly accomplished OSH-RF-1020 Form signed by the employer or authorized representative;
(b) SEC Certificate of Incorporation (for corporations);
(c) DTI Certificate of Business Name Registration (for sole proprietorship);
(d) CDA Certificate (for cooperatives);
(e) Mayor's/Business Permit or Locational Clearance for the establishment;
(f) Detailed layout plan as specified below.

1024.05 Layout Plan Requirements:

Documentation must include detailed floor-by-floor layout plans at 1:100 meter scale showing:
(a) Physical features of the building/establishment;
(b) Storage areas;
(c) Exits and emergency exits;
(d) Aisles and passageways;
(e) Machinery and equipment locations;
(f) Clinic and first-aid stations;
(g) Emergency devices and equipment;
(h) Fire extinguisher locations;
(i) Electrical panel locations;
(j) Hazardous material storage areas.

1024.06 Registration forms may be reprinted, with the reverse side available for additional information.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Failure to register under Rule 1020 may result in:
(a) Administrative fines as provided under RA 11058 and DO 198-18;
(b) Up to P100,000.00 penalty per day under DO 198-18;
(c) Issuance of compliance orders;
(d) Work stoppage orders in cases of imminent danger.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RELEVANT AMENDMENTS AND REFERENCES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This Rule should be read in conjunction with:
- Republic Act No. 11058 (OSH Law)
- DOLE Department Order No. 198-18 (IRR of RA 11058)
- DOLE Department Order No. 252-25 (Revised IRR of RA 11058)
