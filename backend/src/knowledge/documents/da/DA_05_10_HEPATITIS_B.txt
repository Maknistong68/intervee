================================================================================
DOLE DEPARTMENT ADVISORY NO. 05, SERIES OF 2010
GUIDELINES FOR THE IMPLEMENTATION OF A WORKPLACE POLICY AND PROGRAM
ON HEPATITIS B
Effective: January 4, 2010
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Department Advisory No. 05, Series of 2010 (DA 05-10), also known as the "Guidelines for the Implementation of a Workplace Policy and Program on Hepatitis B," serves as the regulation for anything that concerns Hepatitis B at the workplace.

================================================================================
SECTION 1: MANDATORY POLICY REQUIREMENT
================================================================================

It is mandatory for all private workplaces to:
(a) Have a policy on Hepatitis B
(b) Implement a workplace program

================================================================================
SECTION 2: NON-DISCRIMINATION
================================================================================

2.1 General Prohibition:

There shall be no discrimination of any form against workers on the basis of their Hepatitis B status consistent with international agreements on non-discrimination ratified by the Philippines (ILO C111).

2.2 Protected Activities:

Workers shall not be discriminated against, from pre to post-employment, including:
(a) Hiring
(b) Promotion
(c) Assignment
(d) Training opportunities
(e) Benefits
(f) Termination

because of their Hepatitis B status.

2.3 Employment Decision:

Individuals found to be Hepatitis B positive shall not be declared unfit to work without appropriate medical evaluation and counseling.

2.4 Job Security:

Workers shall not be terminated on the basis of the actual, perceived or suspected Hepatitis B status.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: CONFIDENTIALITY
================================================================================

3.1 Disclosure:

Job applicants and workers shall not be compelled to disclose their Hepatitis B status and other related medical information.

3.2 Medical Records:

Hepatitis B-related medical information shall be:
(a) Kept confidential
(b) Stored separately from personnel files
(c) Accessible only to authorized personnel
(d) Not disclosed without worker consent

================================================================================
SECTION 4: VACCINATION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

4.1 Required Vaccination:

The DOLE advisory requires vaccination to all occupations with a conceivable risk of Hepatitis B transmission in the workplace, including:
(a) Health care workers
(b) Other workers whose occupation involves potential exchange of bodily fluids
(c) Workers in high-risk occupations

4.2 Encouraged Vaccination:

All establishments are encouraged to provide Hepatitis B immunization for all its workers.

4.3 Vaccination Coverage:

Hepatitis B vaccination typically involves:
(a) Series of 3 doses
(b) Follow-up testing to confirm immunity
(c) Booster doses if needed

================================================================================
SECTION 5: WORKPLACE PROGRAM COMPONENTS
================================================================================

The workplace Hepatitis B program shall include:

(a) Education and Awareness:
    - Information on Hepatitis B transmission
    - Prevention measures
    - Signs and symptoms
    - Available treatment

(b) Prevention:
    - Vaccination programs
    - Universal precautions
    - Proper handling of sharps
    - Personal protective equipment

(c) Support Services:
    - Access to medical evaluation
    - Counseling services
    - Referral to treatment
    - Continued employment support

================================================================================
SECTION 6: EMPLOYER RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

Employers shall:
(a) Develop and implement Hepatitis B policy
(b) Provide vaccination to high-risk workers
(c) Implement universal precautions
(d) Provide education and training
(e) Maintain confidentiality
(f) Ensure non-discrimination
(g) Provide access to treatment referrals

================================================================================
SECTION 7: HIGH-RISK OCCUPATIONS
================================================================================

Workers at higher risk include:
(a) Healthcare workers
(b) Laboratory personnel
(c) Dental professionals
(d) Emergency responders
(e) Housekeeping staff in healthcare facilities
(f) Workers handling blood or bodily fluids
(g) Waste handlers

================================================================================
SECTION 8: PREVENTION MEASURES
================================================================================

Universal precautions shall include:
(a) Proper hand hygiene
(b) Use of personal protective equipment
(c) Safe handling of sharps
(d) Proper disposal of contaminated materials
(e) Immediate response to exposure incidents
(f) Post-exposure prophylaxis when indicated

================================================================================
SECTION 9: MONITORING AND COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

The DOLE, through its regional offices, in collaboration with:
(a) Department of Health (DOH)
(b) Department of Interior and Local Government (DILG)
(c) Local government units (LGUs)

shall oversee and monitor compliance of private establishments.

================================================================================
SECTION 10: EFFECTIVITY
================================================================================

Implementation became effective on January 4, 2010, fifteen (15) days after the DOLE advisory was published in a major newspaper.

================================================================================
SECTION 11: RELATED ISSUANCES
================================================================================

This Advisory should be read with:
(a) Rule 1960 of OSHS (Occupational Health Services)
(b) RA 11058 (OSH Law)
(c) DOH guidelines on Hepatitis B
(d) ILO Convention 111 (Discrimination)

================================================================================
END OF DEPARTMENT ADVISORY NO. 05, SERIES OF 2010
